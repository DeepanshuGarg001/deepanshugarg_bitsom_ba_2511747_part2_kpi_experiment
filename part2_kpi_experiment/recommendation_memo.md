# Recommendation Memo

**To:** Leadership Team
**From:** Deepanshu Garg,Business Analyst
**Subject:** Analysis and Recommendation for New Onboarding and Activation Campaign

## 1. Executive Summary
We recently conducted an A/B test comparing our existing onboarding experience (Control) against a new onboarding campaign (Treatment). The goal was to improve user conversion and early engagement. The new campaign successfully more than doubled our Paid Conversion Rate from 3.19% to 7.04% (statistically significant). However, it also drove a noticeable increase in support tickets and slightly lower revenue per converted user. Based on these findings, we recommend launching the new campaign to all users, but with an immediate follow-up to address the friction points causing the rise in support tickets.

## 2. North Star Metric
**Paid Conversion Rate** was selected as the North Star Metric for this experiment. Since we operate a subscription-based product, driving users to convert into paying customers is the most direct indicator of business growth and campaign success. Focusing purely on top-of-funnel metrics like landing page visits would not guarantee revenue generation.

## 3. Guardrail Metrics Considered
While the primary goal was to increase conversions, we monitored the following guardrails to ensure the user experience wasn't negatively impacted:
- **Support Ticket Rate:** Increased significantly from 14.8% (Control) to 24.8% (Treatment). This indicates the new onboarding flow might be confusing or broken for some users.
- **Refund Rate:** Increased slightly from 0.0% to 0.42% in the Treatment group. While low, this suggests a minor mismatch in expectations for some users who converted.
- **Average Days to Convert:** Improved (decreased) from 8.86 days in Control to 6.40 days in Treatment, showing the campaign effectively accelerates the activation process.

## 4. Segment-Level Insight
Our segment analysis revealed a few key patterns:
- **By Device:** The Treatment performed exceptionally well on Desktop compared to Control, but the onboarding completion rate improvements on Mobile were less pronounced. This suggests the new mobile flow might need optimization.
- **By Plan Type:** The Treatment successfully pushed more users to start trials for Premium plans, but there is still drop-off during the actual conversion phase.

## 5. Final Recommendation
**Launch** 
We recommend a full rollout of the new onboarding campaign. The positive impact on conversion rate (+3.85 percentage points, a 120% relative lift) and the faster time-to-conversion outweigh the current downsides. The campaign successfully proves that this new approach activates users better than the baseline.

## 6. Risks and Limitations
- **Increased Support Load:** The spike in support ticket rate (from ~15% to ~25%) will strain our customer support team if rolled out to 100% of traffic without fixes.
- **Revenue Mix:** While overall average revenue per user increased, the average revenue *per converted user* dropped in the Treatment group ($704 vs $894). The new campaign might be converting users to lower-tier plans or offering discounts that dilute average order value. 
- **Mobile Experience:** The new flow might not be fully optimized across all screen sizes based on segment data.

## 7. Next Steps
1. **Investigate Support Tickets:** Immediately analyze the support tickets generated by the Treatment group to identify the common points of friction or confusion in the new flow.
2. **Optimize UI/UX:** Push small iterative updates to the mobile experience and clarify any misleading copy that led to the slight bump in refunds.
3. **Monitor AOV:** Keep a close eye on the Average Revenue Per Converted User post-launch to ensure overall profitability isn't negatively impacted long-term.
